# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for participants within the FLASH project community, as well as steps to report unacceptable behavior.

We invite all those who participate in FLASH to help us create safe and positive experiences for everyone.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by the FLASH project, including but not limited to:
- GitHub issues, discussions, and pull requests
- Community chat channels
- Mailing lists
- Events and conferences

## Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Assuming good intent in discussions

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Reporting

If you are subject to or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting the project team at conduct@flash-wallet.io. All reports will be handled with discretion.

Reports should include:
- Your contact information
- Identifying information about the individuals involved
- Description of the behavior and its context
- Any additional context or evidence

### Consequences

After investigation, community leaders may take action including:
- A private warning
- Removal from the community
- Temporary or permanent ban from participation
